Project Management & Planning

Branding Project Plan

For the Boris Schmidt branding project, we created a project plan that covers a branding guide and content strategy. This plan included clear deliverables and a communication strategy. The project plan showed our ability to manage projects professionally and keep the stakeholder informed.

The planning included different phases: research into Boris' music style and target audience, concept development for visual identity, design processes, and implementation of final branding elements. Through this approach, we made sure that all parts of the project were done professionally and that the final results met stakeholder expectations.

Project Phases & Milestones

  • Research Phase: Music style analysis, target audience research, market positioning
  • Concept Development: Visual identity concepts, brand personality definition
  • Design Iteration: Feedback processing, design refinement, stakeholder approval
  • Implementation: Final assets, brand guidelines, delivery

Cardan Project Plan

For the Cardan accessibility project, we created a UX research plan focused on user experience research for people with disabilities. Together with Noortje, I mainly focused on the UX research & interviews part of the project. The plan included research questions and methodologies, interview protocols and a simple user test. This showed our ability to apply research methods and include ethical considerations in the research process.

Initially, we weren't focusing on one specific disability yet, so I conducted additional research into cognitive disabilities to broaden our understanding. Later, we narrowed our focus to visual impairments. The research plan included different research methods: interviews with users with visual impairments, usability testing of existing interfaces, persona development based on real data, and user journey mapping to chart the complete user experience. This approach made sure that all findings were reliable and useful for the design process.

Research Methodology & Documentation

  • User Interviews: Interviews with users with visual impairments
  • Additional Research: Extra research into cognitive disabilities during initial phase
  • Persona Development: Data-driven personas based on real insights
  • User Journey Mapping: Complete user experience mapped out

Research Documentation

  • Interview Documentation: UX interview protocols and findings
  • Cognitive Disabilities Research: Additional research conducted during exploration phase
  • Visual Impairment Focus: Interview results, personas, and user journey for visual disabilities

The Midnight Empress VTuber Pitch

I gave a strategic pitch for "The Midnight Empress" VTuber branding project, presenting my goal and findings so far. This pitch showed my ability to analyze market opportunities, develop brand narratives, and create actionable multi-platform strategies for digital content creators.

Market Analysis & Opportunity

The VTuber space has good opportunities but is very crowded with creators who lack consistent branding or strategic planning. I found that strong identity and strategic approach are key differences that separate successful creators crowd, making memorable connections with audiences important for long-term success.

Brand Identity Development

I created a complete visual identity for "The Midnight Empress" based on audience research and community feedback. The brand centers around themes of mystery, royalty, and elegance, with carefully chosen symbolic elements:

  • Wolf Tail: Represents the character's lore and inner strength
  • Moon Symbol: Reinforces the midnight theme and nocturnal identity
  • Blue Roses: Symbolize rarity, secrecy, and unattainable dreams.

Multi-Platform Content Strategy

I designed a content strategy based on platform analysis and audience behavior research, optimizing for visibility, engagement, and community building:

  • Twitch: Primary platform for livestreams, real-time interaction, and monetization
  • TikTok: Growth engine using short-form content, fast engagement livestreams, and content creation.
  • Discord: Community hub for offline engagement, loyalty building, and deeper fan connections

Teamwork & Professional Tools

Project Organization / Trello

Throughout all group projects, I used Trello boards for project management. These tools enabled organized task management, deadline tracking, and team coordination. Through consistent use of these tools, I made sure that all projects were delivered on time and that all team members knew their responsibilities.

The Trello implementation included different boards for different project phases: planning boards for initial project setup, development boards for ongoing tasks, and review boards for quality assurance. This approach made sure there was transparency in the project process and enabled good collaboration between all team members.

Trello Board Structure

  • Planning Boards: Project setup, initial task breakdown, timeline planning
  • Development Boards: Active tasks, progress tracking, blocker identification
  • Review Boards: Quality assurance, final checks, delivery preparation
  • Archive Boards: Completed tasks, lessons learned, project retrospectives

Team Communication / Discord

For team communication and coordination, I used Discord across all group projects. This platform made digital communication, meeting scheduling, and progress updates between team members work well. Through organized communication channels and regular check-ins, I made sure that all team members stayed engaged and that problems were quickly identified and solved.

The Discord implementation included different channels for different purposes: general project discussions, specific task-related communication, resource sharing, and social interaction to maintain team cohesion. This communication structure helped make all group projects successful.

Discord Channel Organization

  • General Discussion: Project updates, general communication
  • Task Channels: Specific work areas, focused discussions
  • Resource Sharing: Files, links, reference materials
  • Social Channels: Team building, informal communication
